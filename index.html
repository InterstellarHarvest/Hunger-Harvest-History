TAA — FIELD HANDBOOK

WALKING A THREAD

The Temporal Agricultural Archive sends you back along a thread to a moment where the story of food turned — and where the record is about to be sealed with the wrong cause. Your work is always the same: look, ask, gather what really happened, then stabilize the record so history keeps the truth.

YOUR SOURCES

Each scene offers a few ways to learn. Work all of them — and revisit any as often as you like.

PEOPLESpeak with the folk who live it. They answer in their own voice.
THE SCENEExamine the soil, the crop, the ground itself — up close, first-hand.
TAA RECORDSRead a device or archive: instrument readouts and the written record.
TAA COMMSOnce per thread, call your handler for a nudge in the right direction.

TRAVELLING A THREAD

A thread can span more than one place. Use the location bar above the scene to travel. New locations open up as you learn — a locked one is waiting on a clue you haven't found yet. Tap it to see what it needs.

THE RECORD

Every clue you uncover is logged to your Field Notes. Some clues are required to settle the thread; others are extra insight — not needed, but worth points and worth knowing. When you have the required clues, return to the anchor location and Stabilize the Record: make the call, backed by what you found. Guess early if you dare — a wrong call costs you.

READING THE ROOM

People are not interchangeable. Each has a temper — patient, stressed, stoic, curious, and more. Push too hard or too rudely and a mood can close a door on evidence. Some forgive; some don't.

THE CHAIN

The Archive keeps the chain — the unbroken line of how humankind learned to feed itself, from the first saved seed to the last. Break a link (let a moment be remembered wrongly) and everything downstream frays. Each thread you walk keeps one link whole.

RANK LADDER

# 🌾 Hunger, Harvest, & History

*Another Space Sprout Sleuth mystery.*

A retro-pixel edutainment game about the moments that made agriculture possible.

You are a new agent of the **Temporal Agricultural Archive (TAA)** — an institution that guards *the chain*: the unbroken line of how humankind learned to feed itself, from the first saved seed to farms among the stars. That chain is fraying. Threading back through history, you drop into eight pivotal moments, investigate what **truly** happened, and **stabilize the record** before it's remembered wrong.

Every thread is a real turning point in the science and history of food.

## How to play

- **Travel the thread.** Each level lands you in a moment in agricultural history. Some span more than one place — use the location bar to move between them.
- **Investigate.** Talk to the people who lived it, examine the land and the crops, and read the TAA's own instruments and archives.
- **Gather the record.** Clues log to your Field Notes. Some are *required* to settle the thread; others are extra insight worth finding.
- **Stabilize the Record.** When you understand what really happened, make the call — backed by the evidence you gathered. Get it right and history keeps the truth; the chain holds.
- **Climb the ladder.** Settle threads to rise through the TAA's ranks, from *Archive Initiate* to *Keeper of the Chain*.

## The threads

| # | When & where | The turning point |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | TAA Facility · 2387 | Orientation — your first thread |
| 1 | The Fertile Crescent · ~9,700 BCE | The first domesticated grain |
| 2 | Sumer · ~2,000 BCE | Salt and silt — the first soil crisis |
| 3 | Ireland · 1845 | The blight and the Great Famine |
| 4 | Karlsruhe · 1909 | Pulling fertilizer out of the air |
| 5 | The Plains & Washington, D.C. · 1935 | The Dust Bowl and the case for conservation |
| 6 | A vertical farm · 2041 | A living system mistaken for a machine |
| 7 | Home · 2387 | The long yield |

The science in each thread — domestication and selection, soil salinization, monoculture and plant disease, nitrogen fixation, soil conservation, and closed-loop farming — is drawn from the real historical record.

## Running it

The game is a single, self-contained `index.html` — **no build step, no dependencies, no server required.**

- **Play locally:** open `index.html` in a modern browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, or Safari).
- **Host it:** serve the repository folder from any static web host.

Everything the game needs lives alongside it: `hhh_data.js` (all game content) and the `UI/`, `npc/`, and `scene/` asset folders.
